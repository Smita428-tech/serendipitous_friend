Friendship's Serendipitous Journey

In the halls of college, our paths did cross,
A connection formed, a friendship embossed.
She spoke with joy on that first induction day,
Of naphthalene balls, in her own unique way.

I remember it well, her words in the air,
A spark of curiosity, a glimpse of a flare.
I wanted to speak, to know her much more,
Little did I know what the future held in store.

She asked for my help, a drawing request,
But foolishly, I denied, to my own regret.
Perhaps she was hurt, her trust I did break,
Yet fate had a plan, our bond it would make.

Tickets were needed, and she turned to me,
Quiet and reserved, I pondered, should I agree?
Against my nature, I said yes with a smile,
Unbeknownst to me, it would be worthwhile.

The day before the journey, a question arose,
"I have a boyfriend, he'll join us, do you oppose?"
A moment of truth, a test of my heart,
Would I let envy tear our friendship apart?

But I chose friendship, over selfish desire,
Supporting her journey, my soul felt on fire.
For true friends stand by, in both joy and strife,
Embracing each other's unique paths in life.

She often spoke for me when conditions were odd,
Calming the anger, bringing peace, a soothing nod.
She asked me to change rooms, to be closer still,
A chance to deepen our bond, a choice to fulfill.

Regret weighed heavy, as the room shift came near,
I wanted to share a space, to hold her dear.
But self-sabotage held me back, I hesitated,
Creating distance, a void that lingered unabated.

Days drifted by, she met newer faces with glee,
While I held onto memories, longing to be free.
Yet in my heart, her place remained secure,
This poem, a testament, a love that endures.

Eagerly awaiting semester exams, I'd wait,
For she'd invite me, studying together, fate.
Her fluent words, my stammers, complemented so ,
A perfect harmony, as we'd strive and grow.

Her love for activities, participation with zest,
While I focused on studies, feeling I'd done less.
But she taught me to love, to embrace the world wide,
To break free from silence, let my soul inside.

Oh, how she weaved a spell, so ineffably,
Unshackling my heart, setting my spirit free.
I learned to love myself, and this world too,
And most of all, my serendipitous buddy, I love you.

In the tapestry of our college days, so dear,
Destiny wove our stories, forever intertwined, I fear.
From naphthalene balls to room requests and more,
Our friendship's foundation grew stronger than before.

So let this poem be a testament, a fusion of our tales,
Of laughter and tears, of friendship that never fails.
For in those college memories, forever we'll be,
Two souls connected, bound eternally.
